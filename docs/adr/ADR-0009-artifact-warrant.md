## Decision
Every generated artifact carries an artifact warrant — what a green result shows, what it does not show, and what review still covers — proportionate to how non-obvious the bound is, present by construction for committed artifacts and as posture for surfaced renderings. The discipline is an application of the honest-tiers and one-trust-gate principles, not a new principle: it is carried by a glossary term and a change-propagation rule binding every new generated artifact, and it is the third member of the check-honesty family beside the behavioral correlate in the review record and the negative-fixture meta-check. Surfacing the warrant where the operator actually meets the artifact is a named build obligation whose residual is tracked, never claimed already rendered. The guardrail-scoped instance of this discipline lives in the Engine's own guardrail-integrity contract; this record carries the corpus-wide discipline.

## Rationale
Green checks invite silent overclaim: a passing coherence check does not prove the installed modules function, and a passing coverage check does not attest that authored governance content is right. The warrant states each bound plainly — naming an authoring-time judgment as such — instead of letting the operator infer assurance the artifact cannot give, or handing them a review step they cannot perform.

## Anti-choice
Rejected minting a new principle for what existing law already implies, and dressing authored-judgment bounds as operator-reviewable assurance.
