## Decision
The cognitive substrate's documentation grounds itself in the established field rather than implying homegrown novelty: its shape is described in the terms of the established cognitive-architecture taxonomy, its prioritization as context engineering, and its push as active, forward-looking retrieval — cited as vocabulary lineage only, importing no machinery and renaming no engine surface to a standard's terms. A canonical honest-novelty statement bounds the claim: the genuine contributions are the repo-native delivery-and-trust model and two deliberate integration departures — push-not-pull orientation and observe-don't-predict, usage-derived salience. Churning frontier systems are confined to a watch-list, never durable documents. The lineage vocabulary is maintainer-layer only and never surfaces in operator-facing prose or runtime narration.

## Rationale
Uncited framing overclaims novelty and a "nothing new here" framing underclaims the real contributions; both mislead a future builder deciding what is load-bearing. Citing the lineage as taxonomy keeps the field's vocabulary useful without importing machinery the design did not adopt — mapping a standard's categories onto the engine's own surfaces is a category error.

## Anti-choice
Rejected renaming engine surfaces to match the cited standards, claiming no novelty at all, and letting research jargon reach operator surfaces or runtime narration.
