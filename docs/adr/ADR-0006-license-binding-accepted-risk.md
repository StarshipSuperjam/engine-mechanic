## Decision
Whether the template license's source-available restriction binds an adopter's use of the engine code that travels into generated repositories is closed won't-pursue: no engine-scoped license or notice machinery is added, the first-run clearing model is unchanged, and the posture is recorded honestly as declining the enforceability question, not answering it — the restriction may not bind, and that is a tolerated risk. The reopen trigger is commercialization or a concrete enforcement need, at which point a scoped notice remains an available hedge.

## Rationale
For an experimental, no-revenue, sole-maintainer template, commissioning counsel is not warranted, and an eternally parked legal question rots into a phantom dependency that falsely gates downstream work. An honest, eyes-open close removes the false gate while preserving the hedge.

## Anti-choice
Rejected adding notice machinery ahead of any need, and leaving the question open indefinitely. Distinct and untouched: the inverse risk of the template author's copyright wrongly governing an adopter's product.
